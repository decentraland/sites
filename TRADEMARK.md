## What you may do

- **Fork and modify the code.** Fork this repository and build derivative
  works under the Apache 2.0 license.
- **Refer to Decentraland in factual, descriptive contexts.** Examples:
  "This project is compatible with Decentraland", "Imported from the
  Decentraland sites repository", "Tested against the Decentraland protocol".
  Nominative and comparative references that are truthful and non-misleading
  are permitted without prior permission, provided they do not imply
  sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation.
- **Use unmodified screenshots** of the official Decentraland sites for
  journalism, reviews, research, or educational purposes, with attribution
  to "Decentraland".
- **State interoperability and compatibility claims** about your own product
  ("Works with Decentraland wearables", "Imports Decentraland scenes")
  provided the claims are accurate.

## What you may not do without prior written permission

- **Use the Decentraland Marks as your branding.** This includes the names
  **Decentraland**, **DCL**, the Decentraland logo, or any confusingly
  similar name or mark as the name or branding of your own product, service,
  website, application, social media account, or organization.
- **Register look-alike identifiers.** Domain names, social media handles,
  package names, or app store listings that incorporate the Decentraland
  Marks or look-alike variants — for example, substituted characters
  (`d3centraland`), added prefixes/suffixes (`decentraland-pro`), or
  homoglyphs (`decentraIand` with a capital I).
- **Impersonate an official client.** Operate a fork of this codebase, or any
  derivative deployment, in a way that could lead a reasonable user to
  believe it is the official Decentraland website, an official Decentraland
  product, or an official Decentraland service. This is about user-facing
  presentation (branding, copy, domains, UI) — not about which APIs or
  infrastructure your fork talks to.
- **Disparage or defame** the Decentraland Foundation, the Decentraland DAO,
  or their communities while using the Decentraland Marks.

## Forks and clones

You are welcome to fork this repository. If you publish a fork or a derivative
deployment, you must:

1. **Rename the project.** The Decentraland Marks must not appear in the
   project name, repository name, npm/package name, or any deployed domain
   under your control.
2. **Replace brand assets.** Replace the Decentraland logo and brand assets in
   `public/` and `src/` with your own. Remove or replace any references to
   the Decentraland Marks in metadata such as `<title>`, `og:site_name`,
   `manifest.json`, and favicon set.
3. **Add a prominent disclaimer.** State clearly in your README and on any
   deployed site that the project is an independent fork and is **not
   affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by** the Decentraland
   Foundation or the Decentraland DAO.

Forks and derivative projects may use publicly available Decentraland
infrastructure — including public APIs, Catalyst nodes, wallet flows, and
other publicly documented services — in accordance with their applicable
terms and technical limits. This policy does not restrict that use; it only
restricts presenting your project in a way that could mislead users about
affiliation, endorsement, or sponsorship.

## Reporting misuse

If you become aware of a website, application, or account that misuses the
Decentraland Marks or impersonates an official Decentraland product, please
report it to [legal@decentraland.org](mailto:legal@decentraland.org).
Suspected phishing or fraud impacting users should also be reported to
[security@decentraland.org](mailto:security@decentraland.org) so we can act
on the technical side in parallel.

## Requests for permission

For any use of the Decentraland Marks not explicitly permitted above, please
request written permission from
[legal@decentraland.org](mailto:legal@decentraland.org) before using the
marks. Please include the intended use, the audience, and the duration.
